# Acme Health Member Services — Internal Knowledge Base

Acme Health is a not-for-profit integrated health network serving its regional
service area. This knowledge base is used by the **Acme Health Member Services
voice and chat assistant** to answer member questions about pharmacy benefits,
appointments, plan coverage, and the Acme Online portal.

## Pharmacy and Prescriptions

### Prescription Refills
Members can request a refill through three channels: the Acme Online
portal, the Acme Health Member Services phone line (1-800-555-0100), or in
person at any Acme pharmacy.
Refill requests submitted before 2:00 PM Pacific are filled the same business
day. Requests after 2:00 PM are filled the next business day.
Mail-order refills ship within 48 hours and arrive in 5–7 business days via
USPS Priority Mail at no additional charge.
A prescription must have at least one refill remaining and must not be
expired. Acme prescriptions expire 12 months from the original fill date,
or 6 months for Schedule II controlled substances.

### Mail-Order Pharmacy
The Acme Mail-Order Pharmacy fills 90-day supplies of maintenance
medications at the 60-day copay (a 33% savings vs. monthly refills).
Eligible drug classes: cholesterol, blood pressure, diabetes, thyroid,
asthma maintenance, and depression maintenance.
Controlled substances, biologics, and refrigerated specialty medications
are NOT eligible for mail order and must be picked up in person.
To enroll, members go to Acme Online > Pharmacy > Mail-Order Setup,
or call 1-800-555-0100.

### Medication Pricing
The Acme Health Plus formulary has 4 tiers:
- Tier 1 (Preferred Generic): $10 copay 30-day / $20 copay 90-day mail order
- Tier 2 (Generic): $20 copay 30-day / $40 copay 90-day mail order
- Tier 3 (Preferred Brand): $50 copay 30-day / $100 copay 90-day mail order
- Tier 4 (Non-Preferred Brand / Specialty): 25% coinsurance up to $250 max

Members can check the price of a specific medication via Acme Online >
Pharmacy > Price Check, or by asking the Member Services assistant.
The formulary is updated quarterly. Major changes are emailed to affected
members 60 days in advance.

### Prescription Transfers
To transfer an existing prescription from an outside pharmacy to Acme,
the member provides: drug name, current pharmacy name, current pharmacy
phone number, and prescription number.
Transfers complete within 1 business day for non-controlled medications.
Schedule II controlled substances CANNOT be transferred — a new prescription
from the prescribing provider is required.

## Appointments and Provider Access

### Scheduling
Same-day urgent appointments are available at all Acme Health primary
care offices. Routine appointments are typically available within 7–14
days for established patients and 3–4 weeks for new patients.
Members can schedule, reschedule, or cancel appointments through Acme
Online or by calling their local Acme clinic directly.
Cancellations made less than 24 hours in advance may incur a $50 missed
appointment fee for non-emergency visits.

### Telehealth (Acme Video Visits)
Acme offers video visits for established patients on any plan tier.
Video visits cost the same as in-person primary care visits — no
additional charge.
To start a video visit, log in to Acme Online, select your provider,
and click "Start Video Visit." Visits require a smartphone, tablet, or
computer with a camera and microphone.
Same-day video visits with an Acme clinician (not the member's regular
provider) are available 7 AM – 10 PM, 7 days a week through Acme Walk-In
Care.

### Finding In-Network Providers
The Acme network includes 12,000+ providers across its service area.
Members can search by specialty, zip code, language spoken, and whether
the provider is accepting new patients via the Acme Online provider
directory.
Out-of-network providers are covered only for emergency care or with
prior authorization. Members pay the full out-of-network cost otherwise.
Specialist referrals are required on the Acme Health Plus HMO Bronze
and Silver plans. Gold and Platinum plans allow direct specialist access
without a referral.

## Acme Health Plus Plans

### Plan Tiers
Acme Health Plus offers four HMO plan tiers:
- **Bronze**: $0 PCP visit / $75 specialist / $400 ER / Deductible $7,000 / OOP max $9,200
- **Silver**: $10 PCP visit / $50 specialist / $300 ER / Deductible $2,500 / OOP max $7,500
- **Gold**: $0 PCP visit / $35 specialist / $200 ER / Deductible $0 / OOP max $4,500
- **Platinum**: $0 PCP visit / $20 specialist / $150 ER / Deductible $0 / OOP max $2,000

All plans include preventive care at $0, telehealth at no additional cost,
and access to the full Acme network.

### Enrollment and Changes
Open enrollment runs October 15 – December 7 each year. Coverage begins
January 1 of the following year.
Qualifying life events (marriage, birth, job loss, move) trigger a 60-day
Special Enrollment Period during which a member can change plans.
To change plans outside open enrollment, members contact the Acme Health
Plus enrollment team at 1-800-555-0150.

### Coverage Determinations
The Acme Health Member Services assistant explains benefits at a HIGH
LEVEL only. Specific yes/no coverage decisions (prior authorization, claim
appeal, out-of-network exception) require a human Member Services
representative.
Members can request a coverage determination through Acme Online >
Coverage > Request Determination, or by calling 1-800-555-0150.
Standard coverage determinations are made within 14 days. Urgent
determinations (medication needed within 72 hours) are made within 24 hours.

## Acme Online Portal

### Account and Login
Acme Online is the member portal at myonline.acmehealth.example.
Members log in with the email address and password they created at
registration.
Passwords must be at least 10 characters with one uppercase letter, one
number, and one special character. Passwords cannot be reused from the
previous 4 passwords.
After 5 failed login attempts the account is locked for 15 minutes. A
member can also call 1-800-555-0199 to unlock immediately after identity
verification.

### Two-Factor Authentication
Two-factor authentication (2FA) is REQUIRED for all members starting
January 1, 2026.
Supported methods: SMS text code, Microsoft Authenticator, Google
Authenticator, and Authy.
Members can enable 2FA at Acme Online > Account > Security > Set Up
Two-Factor Authentication.
If a member loses access to their 2FA device, they must call 1-800-555-0199
and complete identity verification (date of birth + member ID + last 4 of
SSN) to reset 2FA.

### Features
Acme Online supports:
- Viewing test results (released within 4 days of completion)
- Messaging providers (responses within 2 business days)
- Refilling prescriptions
- Scheduling and joining video visits
- Paying bills and viewing claims
- Downloading immunization records (required for school, work, or travel)

## Billing and Insurance

### Billing
Bills are mailed by the 10th of each month and are due by the 25th.
Members can pay online via Acme Online, by phone at 1-800-555-0100,
by mail, or set up autopay from a bank account or credit card.
A $25 late fee applies to balances unpaid after 30 days. Balances unpaid
after 90 days are sent to collections.

### Insurance Verification
Acme accepts most major insurance plans including: Acme Health Plus,
Aetna, Anthem Blue Cross, Blue Shield of California, Cigna, Health Net,
Kaiser Permanente (limited), Medicare, Medi-Cal, UnitedHealthcare, and
TRICARE.
Members should verify their plan covers a specific Acme facility before
their visit by calling the number on their insurance card.

### Financial Assistance
Acme offers financial assistance to uninsured and underinsured patients
with income up to 400% of the federal poverty level.
Patients can apply online at acmehealth.example/financial-assistance or by
calling 1-800-555-0100. Applications are reviewed within 30 days.
Approved patients receive sliding-scale discounts of 25%, 50%, 75%, or
100% off their bills depending on income.

## Privacy, Identity, and Security

### Identity Verification
Before discussing any member-specific information (prescriptions, test
results, claims, appointments), the Member Services assistant verifies the
caller's identity using THREE factors:
1. Full legal name
2. Date of birth
3. Member ID OR ZIP code on file

The assistant NEVER reveals partial information about a member to an
unverified caller (e.g., "is the date of birth April 15?" — wrong).

### Lockout Policy
After 3 failed identity verification attempts in a single call, the
assistant stops trying and transfers the call to a human Member Services
representative who can verify the caller through additional channels.
This protects against social engineering attacks.

### HIPAA
All conversations with the Acme Member Services assistant are protected
under HIPAA. Audio is retained for 90 days for quality and training,
then permanently deleted. Members can opt out of recording by saying
"please stop recording this call."
